Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know when my meat is properly cooked?

A: Use a meat thermometer to check internal temperatures. Chicken should reach 165°F (74°C), pork 145°F (63°C), and beef varies by preference: 125°F (52°C) for rare, 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare, 145°F (63°C) for medium, and 160°F (71°C) for well-done.

Q: What are some essential kitchen tools for beginners?

A: Start with a good chef's knife, cutting board, mixing bowls, measuring cups and spoons, a skillet, a saucepan, a baking sheet, and a wooden spoon. These basics will handle most cooking tasks and help you build confidence in the kitchen.

Q: How can I make my dishes more flavorful?

A: Season throughout the cooking process, not just at the end. Use fresh herbs when possible, don't forget acid (lemon juice, vinegar), and taste as you go. Building layers of flavor by browning meats and vegetables properly also makes a huge difference.

Q: What's the difference between baking soda and baking powder?

A: Baking soda needs acid to activate and creates lift immediately, while baking powder contains both acid and base and can provide lift twice (when mixed and when heated). They're not always interchangeable, so follow your recipe's specifications.

Q: How do I prevent my pasta from sticking together?

A: Use plenty of salted water (at least 4-6 quarts for 1 pound of pasta), stir the pasta immediately after adding it to the boiling water, and stir occasionally during cooking. Don't add oil to the water - it can prevent sauce from adhering later.

Measurement Conversions

  • • 1 pound = 16 ounces = 454 grams
  • • 1 liter = 1000ml = 4.2 cups (approx)
  • • 350°F = 175°C = Gas Mark 4

Common Substitutions

  • • 1 egg = 1/4 cup applesauce
  • • 1 cup butter = 1 cup coconut oil
  • • 1 cup milk = 1 cup almond milk
  • • 1 tsp vanilla = 1/2 tsp almond extract

Food Safety Tips

  • • Keep hot foods hot (>140°F)
  • • Keep cold foods cold (<40°F)
  • • Wash hands frequently
  • • Don't cross-contaminate

Storage Guidelines

  • • Potatoes: Cool, dark place
  • • Tomatoes: Counter until ripe
  • • Herbs: Like flowers in water
  • • Bread: Room temperature
