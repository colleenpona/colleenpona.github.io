Frequently Asked Questions

General Cooking

Question Answer
How do I know when my pan is hot enough? For most cooking, you can test pan temperature by sprinkling a few drops of water into the pan. If they sizzle and evaporate immediately, the pan is ready. For oil, it should shimmer but not smoke. You can also test with a small piece of food - it should sizzle immediately when it hits the pan.
What's the difference between sautéing and stir-frying? Sautéing uses medium-high heat with a small amount of fat, and food is moved occasionally. Stir-frying uses very high heat with constant movement of ingredients. Stir-frying is typically faster and uses a wok or large skillet, while sautéing can be done in any skillet.
How can I tell if my baked goods are done? Use the toothpick test - insert a clean toothpick into the center. For cakes and muffins, it should come out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. For bread, tap the bottom - it should sound hollow. You can also check if the edges start pulling away from the pan.

Ingredients & Storage

Question Answer
How long do spices stay fresh? Whole spices can last 3-4 years, ground spices 2-3 years, and dried herbs 1-3 years when stored properly in airtight containers away from light and heat. Test freshness by smelling - if the aroma is weak, it's time to replace them.
Should I wash mushrooms before cooking? It's best to clean mushrooms with a damp paper towel or soft brush rather than washing them. Mushrooms are like sponges and absorb water, which can make them soggy when cooked. If you must rinse them, do it quickly and pat them dry immediately.
How do I store fresh herbs? Treat most herbs like flowers - trim the stems and place in water, then cover with a plastic bag and refrigerate. Basil should be stored at room temperature. Hardy herbs like rosemary and thyme can be wrapped in damp paper towels and stored in the refrigerator.

Troubleshooting

Question Answer
My food is burning but not cooking through. What should I do? Lower the heat immediately. This usually means your pan is too hot. You can also cover the pan to trap heat and cook more evenly, or move thicker items to a cooler part of the pan. For baking, you can cover with foil and reduce oven temperature.
How do I fix oversalted food? For soups and stews, add a potato and simmer (then remove), or add more liquid and other ingredients to dilute. For other dishes, add acid (lemon juice, vinegar), sweetness (sugar, honey), or dairy (cream, yogurt) to balance the saltiness. Prevention is key - always taste as you go!
My sauce is too thin. How can I thicken it? Several options: 1) Simmer uncovered to reduce and concentrate, 2) Make a slurry with cornstarch and cold water, 3) Add a roux (butter and flour mixture), 4) Whisk in tomato paste for tomato-based sauces, or 5) Add cream or cheese for cream sauces.

Healthy Cooking

Question Answer
What are some healthy cooking methods? Steaming, poaching, grilling, roasting, and stir-frying with minimal oil are all healthy methods. These preserve nutrients while adding minimal fat. Avoid deep frying and excessive butter or oil. Season with herbs, spices, citrus, and vinegar instead of salt and heavy sauces.
How can I reduce sodium in my cooking? Use herbs, spices, garlic, ginger, citrus juice, and vinegar for flavor instead of salt. Choose low-sodium broths and canned goods. Rinse canned beans and vegetables. Cook from scratch rather than using packaged foods. Gradually reduce salt - your taste buds will adjust.
